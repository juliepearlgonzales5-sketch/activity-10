About the City

A City Rooted in History & Heart

Ozamis City, fondly known as the "Pearl of the North," is a first-class city in Misamis Occidental, Mindanao. Nestled along the shores of Panguil Bay, the city is known for its rich colonial history, vibrant culture, and welcoming people.

From the iconic Cotta Fortress to its thriving food scene, Ozamis blends the old and the new into a destination unlike any other in Northern Mindanao.

Must-See Places

Top Attractions

From heritage sites to pristine beaches — there's always something waiting to be discovered.

Heritage

Cotta Shrine & Fort

A Spanish-era fortress built in the 1700s — one of the few surviving forts in Mindanao. An iconic symbol of Ozamis history and resilience.

Nature & Leisure

Wellness Park

A lush recreational space perfect for morning jogs, family picnics, and relaxation — right in the heart of the city.

Beach

Cotta Beach

A scenic shoreline along Panguil Bay, where locals and visitors come to unwind with stunning water views and golden sunsets.

Resort

Villa Tagalog Beach Resort

A beloved getaway spot near the city with calm waters, cottages by the shore, and the perfect weekend escape vibe.

Dining

Food Park

Ozamis' go-to night spot for local food, street eats, and community vibes. Perfect for a fun evening with friends and family.

Park

Sirang Lente / OSROX Park

A charming urban park with open spaces and relaxing greenery — a community favorite for leisure and local events.

Heritage & Culture

Living History, Vibrant Identity

Ozamis City is a melting pot of Visayan and Maranao influences, shaped by centuries of trade, faith, and community. Its culture is alive in every fiesta, every church bell, and every warm smile from a local.

  • ⛪
    Archdiocese of Ozamis
    Seat of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese — a spiritual and architectural landmark of the city.
  • 🎉
    Sinulog & Local Fiestas
    Colorful festivals celebrating patron saints with street dancing, music, and local delicacies.
  • 🍚
    Local Cuisine
    From kinilaw to kinamatisang manok — Ozamis food is fresh, bold, and uniquely Mindanaoan.
  • 🏛️
    Colonial Architecture
    Walking through the city reveals Spanish-era structures that tell the story of 300+ years of history.

Getting Here

How to Reach Ozamis City

Ozamis City is accessible by air, land, and sea — making it one of the most connected cities in northern Mindanao.

🚐 Bus & Van

Regular bus and van routes connect Ozamis to Cagayan de Oro, Dipolog, and other key cities in Mindanao.

🛣️ Coastal Highway

The scenic coastal road offers a breathtaking drive alongside Panguil Bay — connecting the city to neighboring towns.

✈️
By Air
Ozamis Airport (OZC) with flights from Manila and Cebu
⛴️
By Sea
Ferry routes via Panguil Bay connecting to Lanao del Norte
🚗
By Land
~3 hrs from Cagayan de Oro via Salay coastal highway
